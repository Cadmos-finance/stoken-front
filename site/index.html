From facility to token

See how capital moves through Stoken.

A facility is assessed, tokenized, allocated to verified investors, then serviced through coupon and repayment flows.

01

Facility outline

Facility terms are defined with the issuer: size, tenor, target rate, commodity flow and use of proceeds.

02

Rating assessment

Rating is assigned using Stoken scoring, Moody's RiskCalc, or both before investor capital is committed.

03

Tokenization

The facility is issued as an ERC-20 token structured as a ledger-based security under Swiss law.

04

Allocation

Verified investors allocate capital from whitelisted wallets. Smart-contract rules enforce access.

05

Coupon distribution

Coupon payments are received and distributed to token holders through the platform flow.

06

Repayment at maturity

At maturity, principal repayment returns to investors and the position is closed on-chain.

Investor edge

Built for institutional capital.

USP 01 · Rating

Dual layer credit rating.

Every facility carries a credit rating. Stoken proprietary scoring, independent Moody's RiskCalc, or both.

USP 02 · Origination

Origination quality.

Every issuer is screened by a team with 25+ years of commodity finance experience across JPMorgan, Credit Suisse and BNP Paribas. Credit history, counterparty relationships, loan book and trade flows are reviewed in full before any facility is offered to investors.

USP 03 · Yield

Real-economy yield.

Short-term commodity trade finance facilities with indicative yields of 5–12% and tenors up to 360 days. Uncorrelated to crypto market volatility.

USP 04 · Structure

One Stoken. One facility.

Every Stoken represents a single facility with its own credit rating, yield and maturity. No pooling. No hidden concentration risk. Investors know exactly what they own before committing capital.
